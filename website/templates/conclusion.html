Conclusion

Word vectorization allows a historian to survey textual sources at a volume that would be unimaginable without the assistance of a computer. Comparing the similarity of words over time can guide a researcher to identify and interrogate trends over time at a macro scale.

However, these indications are insufficient to draw a concrete conclusion. Due to the black box nature of neural networks, what each dimension of a word vector represents, and thus the reasons why words are semantically similar, cannot be determined.

Word vectorization can generate intriguing hypothesizes, but a historian needs to further analyze the underlying sources to make sound observations.
